Case Study I

Algorithmic Insider Trading Surveillance

Trade Surveillance

Business Problem

Legacy rule-based transaction surveillance pipelines generated a 98% false-positive rate on insider trading alerts. This massive alert backlog delayed reporting times and forced analysts into repetitive, manual trade audit loops.

Technical Solution

Architected and validated a downstream staging filter utilizing XGBoost. The model ingests raw surveillance alerts alongside historical volume profiles, order book imbalances, and market news vectors to score alert urgency. Explanations were structured using SHAP to ensure model auditability.

Measured Business Impact

Reduced the false-positive alert backlog by 64% while retaining a 100% historical recall rate of actual regulatory inquiries. This system uplift saved over 1,200 analyst clearing-hours annually and expedited regulatory reporting cycles.

Case Study II

Multi-Agent "LLM Council" Validation

Generative AI Validation

Business Problem

The institutional adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs) for KYC and AML search was bottlenecked by model validation cycles. Compliance auditors lacked a scalable, automated framework to verify retrieval outputs (RAG) for hallucinations.

Technical Solution

Designed a multi-agent validation engine using LangGraph. The framework deploys three independent "council" validator agents (representing Anthropic, Google, and OpenAI models) to score KYC outputs on factual alignment. Discrepancies are negotiated by a mediator agent before human sign-off.

Measured Business Impact

Reduced model validation cycle times by 75% and decreased manual reporting labor by 25%. This framework established the department-wide benchmark for LLM-as-a-Judge validation adopted across the MRM division.

Case Study III

Intra-Market Manipulation Model Audit

Model Risk Management

Business Problem

An audit of an intra-market manipulation model (designed to detect spoofing/layering) revealed severe data leakage within look-ahead features and significant model drift. This vulnerability exposed the bank to potential regulatory findings.

Technical Solution

Conducted a mathematical re-engineering of the training matrix to enforce causal temporal splitting. Re-benchmarked the production pipeline against 67 alternative algorithms, validating a robust LightGBM implementation with structured covariance constraints.

Measured Business Impact

Successfully remediated all regulatory findings. The updated LightGBM model improved alert precision by 18%, while the validation methodology led to the institutionalization of a firm-wide performance monitoring framework.
